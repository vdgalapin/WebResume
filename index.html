Childhood

The first thing I remember about the town where I was born is St. Michael’s Cathedral in Iligan City. I grew up in a religious household, and going to church was a regular part of our life. But what really made it special for me as a kid was what came after mass — my grandparents would always buy me orange munchkins (donut holes, as they’re called in the US) from Dunkin' Donuts. I was a kid who loved food a little too much, so I’d always look forward to Sundays and Wednesdays, not just for church, but for those sweet little treats waiting for me afterward.
Eventually, my family moved to Cagayan de Oro — a much bigger city compared to Iligan — because my dad got a new job. I was in 4th grade when we moved, and I stayed there until 8th grade. It was in CDO where I found myself representing our school in chess. I was one of the top players back then, and it’s something I was really proud of. But as much as I loved the game, I knew it wasn’t something that could help me get a scholarship to a private high school — so I had to let that dream go.

Moved to America 2013

Maryville High School

Maryville High School has one of the weirdest mascot stories I’ve ever heard. We were called the Spoofhounds. The name came from our old football coach back in the 80s. At the time, our football team was on a losing streak, and out of frustration, the coach called the players “lazy hounds.” Somehow, that nickname stuck — and that’s how we ended up being called the Spoofhounds. To this day, I still don’t really understand why or how it became our official mascot, but it definitely made us unique.
Throughout my high school life, I did my best to adjust and get along with the new culture and people around me. Coming from the Philippines, moving to an American high school was such a big change. Like many other foreign students, I wanted to be part of that so-called American dream. I took my classes seriously, got involved in clubs and sports, and tried to make the most out of my experience. Eventually, I graduated with honors and received multiple scholarships — proof that all the hard work and effort paid off.

High School Jobs Experience

TACO BELL

One of the best things about working at Taco Bell was getting to keep the food whenever I made a mistake — like if someone ordered a soft taco without cheese, and I had already put cheese on it. I didn’t mind though. More food for me.
I was a machine on the line. I could knock out 12 hard shell tacos in under two minutes — meat, lettuce, and cheddar cheese all perfectly assembled. But out of everything on the menu, my all-time favorite was the steak quesarito. Unfortunately, they discontinued it shortly after I quit — almost like it left with me

CUSTODIAN

I didn’t get a lot of hours working at Taco Bell, especially during the summer. A lot of high school kids were also fighting for hours — and I was one of them. So, I decided to apply for a custodial job at a Catholic church during the summer.
I actually liked working there. They let me play music while I worked, and I could take my lunch break whenever I wanted. I learned to manage my time well because there were always events and activities happening at the church. I had to make sure everything was cleaned and ready before the students and teachers arrived.
I guess I did a good job because they ended up hiring me to clean the school after school hours as a part-time gig too.

Northwest Missouri State University

"Once a Bearcat, always a Bearcat." Sounds like something out of a High School Musical movie, right? Funny enough, that was actually our college motto. I didn’t really join a lot of college activities since I was working two jobs at the time to pay for tuition. It was an okay experience overall—I mostly just came and went to class.

J-SOX Web Application

Description: JSOX is a comprehensive web application designed to streamline and digitize the compliance process for Japan’s version of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (commonly referred to as J-SOX). The platform allows companies to document, assess, and manage their internal controls efficiently. With a user-friendly interface and secure data handling, JSOX enables auditors and compliance officers to generate reports, track process documentation, evaluate risk matrices, and ensure adherence to financial reporting standards. The system was built to support audit transparency, workflow automation, and collaboration across departments, making it a vital tool for governance, risk management, and compliance (GRC).

Technologies: The application was built using LANSA, with frontend components utilizing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. To manage UI behaviors and simplify DOM manipulation, jQuery was used extensively. Data was structured and transformed using XSL and XML, enabling flexible rendering and document handling. The backend logic and database operations were managed through SQL, ensuring efficient data retrieval and storage across modules.

Quality Check

Description: The Quality Check Application is a mobile and web-enabled internal tool built to streamline the quality assurance process across production lines. It allows real-time tracking of quality inspections, defect logging, and performance metrics. Key features include pass/fail assessments, approval workflows, and automatic report generation. Designed for accessibility, the app allows quality staff to perform checks and record results on the go—anywhere in the plant—ensuring continuous monitoring and faster response times to production issues.

Technologies: The application was built using LANSA for cross-platform development (web and mobile). The frontend utilizes HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with jQuery providing enhanced interactivity. XML and XSL handle structured data formatting and dynamic display. Backend operations rely on SQL for database management, and the integrated email system is implemented through LANSA's communication capabilities, enabling automated alerts and notifications.

Warehouse Management System

Description: The Warehouse Management System is a robust web and mobile application developed to manage and streamline end-to-end warehouse operations. The system features comprehensive modules including cycle counting, location verification, wave generation, stock movement, receiving, order picking, and dispatching. Designed for real-time accuracy and operational efficiency, it empowers warehouse staff to execute tasks on the floor using mobile devices—ensuring seamless coordination and faster turnaround times.
One of the key features of the system is its external notification integration, which connects with third-party applications to send alerts and updates to users even when they are offsite or outside working hours. This ensures critical actions—such as urgent stock movements, completed waves, or cycle count discrepancies—are addressed promptly regardless of user location. The system also supports online and offline modes to guarantee uninterrupted functionality across the plant.

Technologies: The WMS was built using LANSA for cross-platform development, supporting both web and mobile interfaces. The frontend was developed using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with jQuery for enhanced interactivity. XML and XSL were used for data formatting and UI presentation. The backend relied on SQL to manage inventory data, transaction logs, and location tracking. For external communication, the system was integrated with third-party notification services via server-side triggers, enabling offsite alerting and continuous operational awareness.gi
